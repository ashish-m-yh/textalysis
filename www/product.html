Textlysis

How it works

Textalysis examines customer feedback from various textual sources and analyses it for positive or negative views expressed by customers and also looks at the major pain-points contributing to negative feedback by a customer.

eg. mention of "poor communication" indicates negative perception about communication with customer.

It performs the analysis by looking for words and phrases from a lexicon built from research in this field. Relative strengths of different words and phrases are accounted for during analysis and negations are handled too. eg. the word 'hate' is a stronger expression than 'dislike'

Our standard lexicon has been derived from lot of research and suffices most needs but can be fine-tuned by you to accommodate common language usage observed in your data-set.

The Technology

Sentiment Analysis is based on the branch of Artificial Intelligence called Natural Language Processing (NLP) which uses a combination of statistical and computer science approaches to 'understand' human language.

There are number of open-source NLP frameworks such as Mallet, OpenNLP, GATE, Weka, Stanford NLP. This application uses Apache OpenNLP, which is a popular Java framework and has well-documented, simple APIs to work with.

Sentiment Analysis Approaches
  1. Corpus-based
    This approach uses a trained model built from an annotated corpus containing rows of data with tagging of sentiment (negative or positive). It is suitable for analysis within a particular domain and needs a significantly wide and representative annotated data-set to achieve high accuracy.

  2. Lexicon-based
    There are different customizable lexicons available on the Web with words and their respective sentiment weights (positive and negative). This is suitable for a general sentiment analysis approach based on popular usage of the English language.

  3. Rule-based
    A set of rules can be defined to determine sentiment words and their polarity. A good approach for very precise extraction of sentiment based on language patterns.

Textalysis uses a combination of lexical and rule-based approaches to provide an accurate representation of sentiment mix in text. Our application does not merely perform a word-based analysis but also phrasal analysis for better results.

Limitations
  1. Probabilistic Science
    NLP, like all machine learning areas is a probabilistic science. We use controlled vocabularies based on reputed online lexicon and corpora and continually improve the quality of these.

  2. Sarcasm Detection
    Sarcasm cannot be detected due to sentence constructions. This is a NLP research field in sentiment analysis and still not mature. We will pursue work on this in future releases.
